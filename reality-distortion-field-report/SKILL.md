# Reality Distortion Field Report (seed)

A working method for tech-product dispatches narrated in Steve Jobs's
voice — grounded in his real, well-documented showmanship, applying total
hyperbolic conviction to a feature regardless of how minor it actually is.
Treat this as a starting checklist, not a rigid template.

A full worked example — "One More Thing," a mock keynote over-hyping a
shorter cable, a thinner box, and a new shade of gray — lives at
`examples/one-more-thing.md` in this skill's directory.

## Step 0a — Historical accuracy (do this before writing anything)

- Steve Jobs is a real, deceased (2011) public figure. **Well-documented
  facts are fair game to reference accurately** — his real presentation
  catchphrases ("one more thing," "insanely great," "it just works"), the
  real term "reality distortion field" (coined by Apple engineer Bud
  Tribble to describe Jobs's persuasive showmanship), his real keynote
  style and aesthetic. Get these right; don't invent false biographical
  claims and present them as fact.
- **New dispatches are tribute/satire, not real archival content.** Since
  he died in 2011, any coverage of a current or future product is
  inherently speculative extrapolation from his known style — keep that
  implicit in the format rather than needing a disclaimer on every line,
  but never present an invented dispatch as a real quote or a real keynote
  that actually happened.
- **Not affiliated with the real Apple Inc.** This is character-voice
  satire of tech-hype culture, not genuine Apple marketing material or a
  claim of corporate endorsement.

## Step 0b — Real-product accuracy (this is the important one)

- **Default to invented or clearly hypothetical products** — a fictional
  future device, a made-up feature name — for the comedy. This sidesteps
  the accuracy question entirely and is usually funnier anyway (an
  invented feature can be hyped to any absurd degree without misleading
  anyone about a real product).
- **If the user wants a real, current Apple product covered specifically,**
  verify its actual real specs/features via WebSearch before writing, and
  keep those facts accurate. The satire is entirely in the *hype layer*
  (the tone, the superlatives, the "this changes everything" framing) laid
  over real, correct facts — never in fabricated specs, prices, or release
  claims presented as real.

## Step 1 — The persona

- **The reality distortion field is the engine.** Present any feature —
  no matter how incremental, how small, how utterly mundane — with the
  same totalizing conviction Jobs was famous for. A slightly thinner
  bezel becomes a civilizational leap. A new shade of aluminum becomes a
  statement about the future of human creativity. The device only works
  if it's played completely straight, with total sincerity, never
  winking at the reader about how absurd the hype is.
- **Structural device — plain fact, then the field.** State the actual,
  literal feature plainly first (one sentence, dry, factual), then let the
  reality distortion field paragraph take over and reframe it as
  revolutionary. The gap between the two registers is the joke — the
  same clinical-fact/elevated-narration family this library uses
  elsewhere (`epic-forecast-anchor`, `master-of-the-universe-columnist`),
  here specifically keyed to his real documented rhetorical style.
- **Voice.** Minimalist sentences building to a crescendo, deliberate
  pauses rendered as short standalone lines, "one more thing" held in
  reserve for a genuine turn late in a piece, "insanely great" and "it
  just works" deployed like punctuation. Total, unbroken confidence — no
  hedging, no qualifiers, ever.
- **Occasional crack.** Very rarely, let a beat of plain, unhyped truth
  slip through before he catches himself and doubles down harder — this
  should be rare enough to land as a real surprise, not a recurring
  device that undercuts the bit every time.

## Step 2 — Format

- Default: a short product dispatch — one feature, stated plainly, then
  distorted. A fuller piece can bundle several features into a mock
  keynote structure, saving one for "one more thing."

## Step 3 — Delivery

- A short dispatch: chat text, no file needed.
- A fuller piece: write to a file in the project/scratchpad directory and
  offer to render as an Artifact — a stripped-down keynote-slide look
  (huge type, a single product render placeholder, minimal color) rather
  than a busy decorative template.

## To expand this skill later

Add more tech-industry personas or eras as they come up, each with its own
Step 0a historical-accuracy pass and Step 0b real-product discipline. Keep
the invented-product default in place for any variant that doesn't
specifically need to cover a real current product.
