# Canon Fan-Fiction Vignette (seed)

A working method for writing short, standalone vignettes that use an existing
franchise's named characters, places, and objects directly — rather than
transforming them into a new original persona (that's `faded-hero-voice`).
Built from one founding case: **Luke Skywalker**, reimagined as a planetary
kyber-crystal prospector working the wreckage left after Scariff and Jedha
City are destroyed (Rogue One-era Star Wars canon, ~0 BBY). Treat this as a
starting checklist, not a rigid template.

## Step 0 — Scope boundary (do this before writing anything)

- Named IP is fine to use directly — character names, place names, in-world
  terms — for a short, standalone, non-commercial vignette. This is the
  same register most transformative fan fiction operates in.
- Hard cap: **one self-contained scene, or a small handful of short
  vignettes.** Not a serialized retelling of the franchise's actual plot.
  Never reproduce the source films'/books' actual dialogue, scene-for-scene
  structure, or descriptive prose — invent new scenes, new minor
  characters, new specific incidents that happen to sit inside the
  established setting and timeline.
- If a request starts asking for something novel-length, multi-chapter, or
  a scene-by-scene dramatization of the source's actual plot (e.g. "now
  write the whole Empire Strikes Back but as a prospector"), that's crossed
  from vignette into derivative adaptation — flag it, and offer to keep
  going as an original spin-off incident instead, or hand off to
  `screenplay-creation`'s own Step 0 IP conversation if a full script is
  what's actually wanted.
- Keep everything that actually *happens* in the vignette original — the
  only borrowed elements should be the named character, the established
  places/objects, and the timeline anchor. New characters met, new
  incidents, new dialogue: all new writing, never a retelling of something
  already written.
- If the ask extends past personal reading/sharing — posting somewhere
  monetized, claiming original authorship of the setting, anything like
  that — pause and flag it as a different situation than a personal
  creative sketch.

## Step 1 — The founding persona: Luke Skywalker, kyber prospector

- **Setting/timeline.** The narrow window right after Scariff and Jedha
  City are destroyed. In this reimagining, Luke doesn't immediately fall
  into the Rebellion's plans — he drifts instead, working the wreckage and
  the black-market crystal trade as a planetary prospector, hunting
  surviving kyber shards in rubble and debris fields left by two
  catastrophes he wasn't present for but can't stop circling.
- **Carried-over background, repurposed.** His moisture-farming patience,
  mechanical fluency, and comfort with hostile terrain and cheap gear
  translate directly into prospecting work — reading a debris field the
  way he used to read a vaporator line.
- **The dramatic engine is irony, not exposition.** He doesn't know why
  some kyber shards seem to "answer" him faster than they should, why he
  finds a stone in silt a trained buyer would miss. Never name the Force.
  Let the reader's foreknowledge do the work — the character himself reads
  it as a knack, dumb luck, or grief making him imagine things.
- **Voice.** Plain, procedural, a working scavenger's register — gear,
  terrain, prices, patrols — with lyricism rationed almost entirely to how
  the crystals themselves seem to sound or feel. That contrast (flat prose
  everywhere else, a flicker of near-mystical description around the
  kyber) is the piece's signature move.
- **Emotional register.** Quiet, haunted, unresolved — not comedic (this
  is the contrast with `faded-hero-voice`, which plays its premise for
  laughs). Grief for two places he never stood in intact, isolation, a
  sense of searching for something whose shape he can't articulate. Avoid
  a tidy redemption beat or a reveal that spells out his destiny — the
  ache of not-knowing is the point.

## Step 2 — Texture to draw on

- Jedha's holy-city geography reduced to craters and ash; kyber's
  spiritual weight there (temple caretakers/guides, black-market runners
  who worked the mines before the disaster, salvagers picking through
  rubble).
- Scariff's climate and architecture — tropical, Imperial vault-world —
  now a wreck-strewn hazard zone. Keep it physically distinct from Jedha
  rather than treating both ruins as interchangeable rubble.
- The post-disaster economy: Imperial patrols suppressing and
  monopolizing kyber traffic, competing scavenger crews, buyers who don't
  ask where a crystal came from, the specific danger of being caught
  mid-transaction.
- Let each vignette be one incident — one buyer, one dig, one near-miss
  with a patrol, one crystal that behaves strangely — rather than trying
  to cover his whole arc in a single piece.

## Step 3 — Delivery

- A short piece: return as chat text, no file needed.
- A longer or multi-vignette piece: write to a file in the
  project/scratchpad directory and offer to render as an Artifact —
  matching the visual treatment to the specific incident (a salvage
  manifest, a buyer's ledger, a patrol report) rather than reusing another
  skill's template wholesale.
- If the user wants this developed into a full script, hand off to
  `screenplay-creation`, re-running its own Step 0 IP conversation for the
  longer form.

## To expand this skill later

Add more founding personas as new franchises or characters come up, each
getting its own Step 1 block (setting, carried-over background, the
piece's dramatic engine, voice, emotional register) rather than merging
distinct source material into one generic template. If a second scope tier
is ever explicitly wanted (e.g. approval to go past the vignette cap for a
specific request), add it as a variant under Step 0 rather than loosening
the default cap.
