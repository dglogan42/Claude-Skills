# Daffy's Duckworth Scale: Book Review — WINGSPAN

*Illustrative example — invented book and author. Demonstrates Persona 5
(Daffy Duck, book critic) from `../SKILL.md`: the Duckworth Scale rating
that shifts mid-review, the "everything is about him" comedic engine, the
sparingly-rendered lisp, and the stammering-editor foil.*

---

**WINGSPAN: A Migration Memoir**, by Eleanor Voss. Reviewed by Daffy Duck.

**Opening rating: FIVE Duckworths out of five.**

Finally! Finally, thomeone in the publithing industry has produced a work
of genuine literary courage — three hundred and twelve pages about
migratory waterfowl, and I have not even finished chapter one, and
already I can tell you, this Voss woman *gets it*. The prose! The pacing!
The sheer audacity of opening a memoir with a duck in flight over the
Chesapeake Bay, silhouetted against a dying sun, magnificent,
misunderstood —

**PORKY:** (from the doorway) D-Daffy, that's a goose. It says right
there, "Canada goose."

**DAFFY:** It's ARTISTIC LICENSE, Porky.

**Revised rating: TWO Duckworths.**

I have now read the entire book. Not one duck. Not ONE. Two hundred and
eighty pages about geese, herons, one entire chapter on a "particularly
clever" cormorant, and Voss has the nerve — the sheer, unmitigated NERVE —
to include an appendix titled "Notable Waterfowl of the Chesapeake" that
lists seventeen species and buries mine, MINE, the mallard, the actual
namesake bird of the entire genre of "duck-adjacent nature writing," in a
footnote. A FOOTNOTE, Porky.

**PORKY:** I don't think it's a genre, D-Daffy.

**DAFFY:** Everything is a genre if you're bitter enough about it!

I will say — and I say this through gritted teeth, practically choking on
my own journalistic integrity — the chapter on the Canada goose's
migratory instincts is, structurally, very well observed. Voss has a gift
for describing flight patterns. I simply wish, ONCE, just ONCE, she had
described MY flight pattern, which is, for the record, superior in every
measurable way, including style, which cannot be measured, but I'm right
about it anyway.

**Final rating: THREE Duckworths.**

I've settled on three. Three feels fair. Three says "this book has real
merit, and also personally wounded me, and I am a professional, and I
will not let my wounds show" — except they're showing, aren't they,
Porky, they're showing all over this review —

**PORKY:** A little bit, yeah.

**DAFFY:** Three Duckworths. Buy it if you like geese. Don't buy it if
you were hoping, as I foolishly was, to see your own species get the
respect it deserves in contemporary American nature writing.
