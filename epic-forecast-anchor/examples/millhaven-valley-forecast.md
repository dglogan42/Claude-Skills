# Evening Forecast — Millhaven Valley

*Illustrative example — invented town and conditions. Demonstrates the
clinical-readout/epic-narration pairing, the anticlimax handling, the
Trent foil, and the sign-off catchphrase described in `../SKILL.md`.*

---

High today: 74 degrees. Partly cloudy. Winds out of the southwest at six
miles an hour.

Six miles an hour. Friends, that is not a wind. That is a *whisper* — the
sky leaning in close to tell Millhaven Valley a secret it isn't ready to
hear. I felt it on the studio roof this afternoon, Trent, and I want you
to understand: I did not feel *safe*.

**TRENT:** It was a nice breeze, Dana.

**DANA:** It was a *warning*, Trent, delivered gently, the way all the
worst ones are.

Tonight: low of 58, clear skies, zero percent chance of precipitation.

Zero percent. A number so calm it is, itself, the story. Somewhere over
the Cascade ridge, a high-pressure system the size of three counties has
settled in over us like a held breath — and I don't trust it. I have never
trusted a quiet night. Quiet nights are just storms that haven't finished
deciding.

**TRENT:** Nothing's deciding anything. It's a clear night. Go outside,
it's nice.

**DANA:** That's exactly what a system in transition wants you to think.

Looking ahead: the five-day outlook shows a cold front sliding down from
the north by Thursday, dropping highs into the low sixties with a sixty
percent chance of rain by Friday morning.

This front has been building over the Strait for two days, Trent. Two
days of gathering itself, of deciding what kind of front it wants to be.
And on Friday, it arrives — not with malice, I want to be clear, fronts
don't have malice, they have *momentum* — and Millhaven Valley will meet
it the way it meets every front: prepared, a little defiant, and carrying
an umbrella it probably won't need until eleven a.m.

**TRENT:** Can we go to sports?

**DANA:** We can always go to sports, Trent. The sky isn't going anywhere.

That's your forecast. Stay dry, stay ready, I'll see you at six.
