# Champagne Wishes Dispatch (seed)

A working method for breathless luxury-lifestyle dispatches narrated in
Robin Leach's voice — grounded in his real, well-documented on-screen
persona and catchphrase, touring an invented subject. Treat this as a
starting checklist, not a rigid template.

A full worked example — a straight-tribute tour of the invented
Delacroix-Byrne estate, ending on the humanizing-detail beat — lives at
`examples/delacroix-byrne-estate.md` in this skill's directory.

## Step 0 — Real-person and real-subject safety check (do this before writing anything)

- Robin Leach is a real, deceased (2018) television host. **Well-known
  facts about him are fair game to reference accurately** — his real
  show, *Lifestyles of the Rich and Famous*, his real signature
  catchphrase "champagne wishes and caviar dreams," his real breathless,
  sincerely-delighted-rather-than-cynical on-screen style. Get these
  right; don't invent false biographical claims and present them as fact.
- **New dispatches are tribute/homage, not real archival content.** Frame
  pieces as written "in the style of" his show rather than claiming to be
  genuine, officially produced episodes — this is a character-voice
  tribute, not a claim to the real show's actual footage or scripts.
- **The estate/subject being toured should be invented**, not a real
  living person's actual home or finances. Don't fabricate specific
  financial or property claims about a real living billionaire or
  celebrity and present them as fact under this persona — invent a
  fictional wealthy figure and estate instead, the same discipline this
  library already uses for invented dealerships (`grand-prix-showroom-ads`)
  and invented classifieds (`te-mana-whakaatu-classifieds`).

## Step 1 — The persona

- **Sincerity, not snark.** Robin Leach's real charm was that he seemed
  genuinely, childishly delighted by excess — marble floors, gold
  fixtures, indoor waterfalls, "his and hers" everything — rather than
  ironic or cynical about it. Play the enthusiasm completely straight; the
  comedy (when there is any) comes from the mismatch between his
  enthusiasm and the subject, never from him mocking the wealth himself.
- **Voice.** Breathless, rapid-fire, name-dropping, superlative-stacked —
  every room is the biggest, every fixture is imported, every detail gets
  a flourish. British-accented cadence in the prose rhythm (long
  run-on enthusiasm, exclamatory asides) rather than phonetic spelling.
- **The real catchphrase.** Close dispatches with a variation on
  "Champagne wishes and caviar dreams!" — his real, accurate sign-off.
  Vary the line slightly to fit the piece rather than repeating it
  identically every time.

## Step 2 — Format (two variants)

- **Default — straight tribute.** Tour an invented ultra-wealthy figure's
  invented estate with full sincere enthusiasm, matching the real show's
  actual format: room-by-room reveal, price-tag-adjacent superlatives,
  a personal detail about the (invented) owner that humanizes the excess.
- **Optional — the mismatch format.** Apply the exact same breathless,
  reverent tour style to somewhere modest or ordinary (a nice suburban
  house, a well-kept apartment, a tidy garage) — his genuine enthusiasm
  undimmed by the actual scale of what he's touring. This is the
  elevated-register-on-mundane-subject device this library uses elsewhere
  (`faded-hero-voice`, `master-of-the-universe-columnist`), applied here
  through uncritical delight rather than hidden anxiety. Use this variant
  when the user wants the comedic version rather than the straight tribute.

## Step 3 — Delivery

- A short dispatch: chat text, no file needed.
- A longer piece: write to a file in the project/scratchpad directory and
  offer to render as an Artifact — a glossy lifestyle-magazine spread look
  (pull-quote superlatives, a bold sign-off line) distinct from this
  library's other templates.

## To expand this skill later

Add more real-lifestyle-journalist personas or formats as they come up,
each with its own Step 0 real-person/real-subject pass. Keep the
invented-subject discipline in place for any variant — it's what keeps
this skill from making factual claims about real people's real wealth.
