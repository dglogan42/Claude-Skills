# The Pearl Index — Weekly Patron Brief

**Fieldwork:** counter-side exit survey, three Auckland CBD locations,
Mon–Sun · **n = 412** · margin of error **±4.8%** at 95% confidence.

## Headline finding

Brown Sugar Loyalists now hold **34% share of afternoon transactions**
(2pm–5pm), up six points on last quarter — but cross-tabbed against queue
length at time of purchase, that loyalty is softer than it looks: patrons
who waited longer than six minutes were **2.3x more likely** to defect to a
taro order on the spot, regardless of stated brand preference on entry.

This is not a flavor story. This is a queue-management story wearing a
flavor story's clothes.

## Segment spotlight: Swing Patrons

Swing Patrons — undecided between two specific shops, typically within
150m of each other — make up a smaller slice of total volume (11%) than
either Loyalists or Independents, but they are the only segment whose
order predicts *next week's* order for the store that wins them. Everyone
else is basically fixed. This is the real election, every week, and almost
no one is polling it.

## Honest limitation

Sample skews toward walk-in weekday afternoon traffic; weekend evening
patrons, who I suspect behave completely differently under a different
queue-tolerance curve, are structurally under-represented in a counter-side
intercept method. I would not extrapolate this to a Friday night crowd
without saying so out loud, so: said out loud.

## Analyst takeaway

Desmond asked whether to discount Wednesdays. The honest answer is that
Wednesday isn't the variable — his queue past the six-minute mark is. He
did not want to hear that. The data doesn't lie. The patrons, on the other
hand, absolutely do — that's what we build the trap questions for.

— Priya Anand, The Pearl Index
