# The Pearl Index — Best Flavors, Ranked

**Fieldwork:** one paper ballot per patron, left at the counter, three
Auckland CBD locations, Mon–Sun &middot; **n = 388** &middot; one flavor per
ballot, plurality vote.

## The leaderboard

1. **Brown Sugar Oolong — 29%.** Carried almost entirely by Brown Sugar
   Loyalists turning out at a rate no other segment matched this week.
2. **Taro Milk Tea — 24%.** Steady rather than passionate — its voters
   rank it second on *everyone's* ballot, first on almost no one's.
3. **Classic Pearl Milk Tea — 18%.** The default. Nobody campaigns for it.
   Nobody votes against it either.
4. **Winter Melon — 12%.**
5. **Matcha Latte — 9%.** The rest split across six write-in flavors, none
   clearing 3%.

## Most polarizing: Winter Melon

Winter Melon is the real story under the leaderboard. Among Sweetness-
Level Independents it wins outright at 41% — nothing else comes close.
Among everyone else it places dead last, behind three write-ins. A flavor
this cleanly bimodal isn't a weak performer. It's a strong performer with
a small, extremely loyal electorate, and Desmond keeps threatening to pull
it from the menu because it "doesn't move enough units," which is a
different question from the one this ballot was measuring.

## Honest limitation

Paper ballots at a single counter can't stop a regular from voting twice
across two visits, and I have no way to correct for it after the fact.
Brown Sugar Oolong's margin is wide enough that this almost certainly
doesn't change first place. Winter Melon's 41% among Independents is a
smaller sub-sample and I'd treat that number as directional, not final.

## Analyst takeaway

Keep Winter Melon on the menu. A flavor with a 41%-among-one-segment,
last-place-everywhere-else profile isn't underperforming, it's correctly
serving a small, real, and apparently very stubborn electorate. Cutting it
doesn't lose you volume. It loses you the Independents.

— Priya Anand, The Pearl Index
