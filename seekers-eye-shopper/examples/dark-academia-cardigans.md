# Cho's Finds — Dark Academia Cardigans

*Illustrative example — search guidance, not verified current listings.
Prices, sellers, and exact links below are placeholders for what to look
for, not real inventory. Written to demonstrate the "Seeker's eye" device
and Step 0b honesty discipline described in `../SKILL.md`.*

---

I nearly scrolled past all of it. Forty listings deep into "oversized knit
cardigan," and thirty-nine of them are the same three stock photos wearing
the same three faces, which tells you nothing except that somebody found a
supplier and stopped looking. You have to slow down for the one that
doesn't match the pattern — that's usually the one worth a second look.

**1. The one with real reviews, not just real photos.** Look for a
ribbed-knit oversized cardigan, "professor" cut — dropped shoulder, patch
pockets, hits mid-thigh. What made it the find: the listing had customer
photos, not just the seller's studio shots, and three separate reviewers
mentioned the sleeves running long, which is either a flaw or exactly the
point depending on what you're going for. *Check before buying: read the
size chart in centimeters, not the S/M/L label — AliExpress sizing runs
small and inconsistent seller to seller.*

**2. The quiet one, buried on page three.** A merino-blend crewneck in a
deep moss or burgundy — not the cardigan itself, but worth pairing with
one, since a good cardigan needs something underneath that isn't fighting
it for attention. What made it the find: a seller with a consistent
multi-year rating rather than a shop that popped up six months ago with
five hundred five-star reviews and no photos.

**3. The accessory nobody searches for by name.** A brass or antiqued-gold
brooch/pin, small enough to actually wear daily rather than sit in a
drawer. What made it the find: it read as costume-adjacent in the
thumbnail and genuinely wearable in the actual product photos — that gap
is exactly where the good ones hide, because the bad ones photograph
exactly as bad as they are.

**Skip this one.** The "vintage tweed blazer" a few rows over — the one
with forty thousand orders and a price that's suspiciously lower than the
wool-blend listings around it. Read the material line closely: it says
"polyester" once, in small print, under a photo doing everything it can to
look like tweed. Not a scandal, just not what it's dressed as. Skip it if
you actually want the weight and drape a blazer like that promises.

**Seeker's verdict:** the cardigan and the pin. Buy those two and you've
got the actual silhouette; everything else in that search was decoration
around it.
