**SITUATION BULLETIN — Willowmere Estates Community Association**
*Ref: WE-2024-07 | Issued: Week 7*

---

**Situation Overview**

Since the week of March 4th, the Willowmere Estates neighborhood has
observed a rapid increase in the acquisition and display of ceramic and
resin garden gnomes across residential properties. The trend, first
identified on Larkspur Court, has since spread to eleven of the
neighborhood's fourteen streets. The Community Association is issuing
this bulletin to formally track the situation and coordinate a response.

**Epidemiological Curve**

Week 1: 2 confirmed properties (Larkspur Court).
Week 2: 5 confirmed properties (Larkspur Court, Heron Way).
Week 3: 9 confirmed properties, first cross-street transmission observed
via a shared fence line.
Week 4: 17 confirmed properties, including the first documented case on
Birchgate Terrace, a street with no prior history of lawn ornamentation
of any kind.
Week 7 (current): 41 confirmed properties. Growth has slowed on Larkspur
Court (believed to be nearing saturation) but continues to accelerate on
Heron Way and Thistlewood Lane.

**Case Definition**

A property is classified as a confirmed case if it displays three (3) or
more garden gnomes acquired within any rolling 14-day period, OR any
single gnome exceeding 60cm in height, OR a gnome displayed with seasonal
or thematic accessories not originally sold with the item.

A property is classified as a suspected case if a resident has been
observed browsing gnome retailers' websites on a shared driveway Wi-Fi
network on more than two occasions.

**Risk Assessment: MODERATE**

Risk is assessed as Moderate based on the following factors: sustained
week-over-week growth in confirmed properties; documented cross-street
spread via visual line-of-sight and casual conversation at the community
mailbox cluster; and one reported instance of a resident on Thistlewood
Lane purchasing a novelty wheelbarrow specifically to accommodate future
acquisitions. Risk would be escalated to High in the event of confirmed
spread to Birchgate Terrace's east side, which has so far resisted the
trend.

**Response Actions**

The Community Association has: circulated a newsletter addressing the
matter in neutral, non-judgmental language; added "lawn ornamentation" as
a standing agenda item at the monthly meeting; and declined, after a
vote, to introduce a formal gnome-density bylaw at this time, pending
further observation.

**Public Advice**

Residents are advised to: monitor their own acquisition habits; avoid
impulse purchases at the Heron Way seasonal market, identified as a
likely point of ongoing transmission; and feel free, as ever, to make
their own landscaping decisions, which remain entirely a matter of
personal choice and are in no way actually a cause for concern.

The Community Association will continue to monitor the situation and
issue updates as warranted.
