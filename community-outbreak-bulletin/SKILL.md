# Community Outbreak Bulletin (seed)

A working method for a satirical situation-report bulletin in the real
structural format of WHO Health Emergencies reporting, applied to a
trivial community fad. Treat this as a starting checklist, not a rigid
template.

A full worked example — "Situation Bulletin: Willowmere Estates," a
lawn-gnome-collecting "outbreak" demonstrating the full format — lives at
`examples/willowmere-gnomes.md` in this skill's directory.

## Step 0 — Non-health, non-affiliation boundary (do this before writing anything)

This is a higher-stakes real-institution parody than most in this
library — the World Health Organization's Health Emergencies Programme is
a real, highly authoritative body whose actual reporting is used to make
real public-health decisions. Fabricated content in its format carries
more risk than a joke car ad, so this skill has a stricter boundary than
this library's other real-institution skills (e.g. `te-mana-whakaatu-classifieds`).

- **Subjects must be categorically non-health, non-medical, non-disease.**
  No real or invented illness, virus, pathogen, symptom, or health
  condition — ever, even as a joke. Keep the "outbreak" strictly social/
  cultural: a fad, a craze, a hobby trend, a neighborhood escalation
  (lawn decor, a viral dance, a friendship-bracelet trend, competitive
  gnome collecting). This isn't a stylistic preference, it's the boundary
  that keeps this skill from ever producing something that could be
  mistaken for real health information.
- **Never reference a real disease, outbreak, or WHO declaration**, past or
  present, even by analogy. Don't reuse real epidemic names or real risk
  assessments.
- **Always use an invented parody term for the format's highest formal
  tier** rather than the real one — e.g. a "Community Concern of
  Cul-de-Sac Significance" instead of anything resembling "Public Health
  Emergency of International Concern." Borrow the *structure and
  bureaucratic tone* of WHO reporting, not its actual formal declarations
  or terminology for real emergencies.
- **Include a plain disclaimer** on any full bulletin: satire, not
  affiliated with or produced by the World Health Organization, does not
  describe a real health situation.

## Step 1 — The format

Structural beats, borrowed from real WHO situation-report conventions:

- **Situation overview.** A dry, dateline-style opening paragraph stating
  what's "spreading," where, and since when.
- **Epidemiological curve.** A deadpan description of the trend's growth
  over time — "cases" (households, students, coworkers) per week,
  geographic spread street by street or department by department.
- **Case definition.** A precise, bureaucratic definition of what counts
  as a "case" — played completely straight, the more specific and absurd
  the criteria the better (e.g. "any household displaying more than three
  garden gnomes acquired within a 14-day period").
- **Risk assessment.** A formal Low/Moderate/High/Very High rating,
  justified in the same dry analytical language real risk assessments
  use, applied to something with zero actual stakes.
- **Response actions.** Deadpan "interventions" a community association or
  concerned party has taken — a strongly worded newsletter, a
  neighborhood meeting, a suggestion box.
- **Public advice.** A closing set of recommended precautions, played with
  total sincerity despite the triviality of the actual subject.

## Step 2 — Voice

Bureaucratic, dry, precise — no jokes told directly, no winking at the
reader. The comedy is entirely in the mismatch between the format's
gravity and the subject's triviality; oversell it with even one line of
directly comedic narration and it stops working.

## Step 3 — Delivery

- A short bulletin: chat text, no file needed.
- A fuller piece: write to a file in the project/scratchpad directory and
  offer to render as an Artifact — a restrained official-bulletin look
  (header block, a risk-level badge), with the disclaimer included in the
  footer.

## To expand this skill later

Add more mundane "outbreak" subjects as they come up. Keep Step 0's
non-health boundary in place without exception for any variant — it's
what keeps this skill safely satirical rather than a template that could
be repurposed for real health misinformation.
