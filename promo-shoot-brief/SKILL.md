# Promo Shoot Brief

Converts a sequence of physical poses or movements into a production-ready shot list for a photo/video shoot. Reusable across any source material: yoga routines, dance warm-ups, fitness sequences, choreography breakdowns.

## Hard rule: talent stays generic

Direction is always written for **"the performer(s)"** or **"the talent"** — never for a specific named, real, identifiable person (no real celebrities, artists, or individuals by name), unless the user has stated clear authorization from that person, their agent, or their representation. If a shoot is being built around a real act or public figure without stated authorization, decline to attribute direction to them by name and offer the generic-talent version instead — casting is assigned separately from the brief.

Keep direction physically neutral and non-sexualizing: describe pose, framing, and mood, not the performer's body or appearance.

## Output structure

**Header**
- One-line concept/kicker (format: stills / vertical video / both)
- Title
- One short paragraph: the concept, tone, and what the sequence is being adapted from
- Meta grid: format, talent count, number of setups, estimated runtime

**Shot list** — one entry per pose/movement in the source sequence, in order:
- Shot number (tabular, sequential)
- Working title (a short evocative name for the beat, not just the pose name) + source pose in small type
- Framing (camera angle, distance, depth of field)
- Lighting (one clear direction — source, hardness, mood)
- Direction (what the performer physically does, written as action, not appearance)
- Hold (duration, rep count, or "stills only")
- Optional: sound/audio note if the deliverable includes video

Vary framing and lighting across the list — don't repeat the same angle or light setup back to back. Let the emotional arc of the source sequence (e.g., calm → dynamic → calm) drive shifts in energy: harder light and lower angles for higher-effort poses, soft/even light for restful ones.

**Notes section** (closing card): wardrobe (generic, unbranded unless specified), continuity flags (any pose done on both sides), deliverables (stills count + video cutdown length), audio/production notes.

## Design treatment

Utilitarian production-document styling — this is a working document, not a poster. Real typographic hierarchy and a functional palette (one accent color used for shot numbers/labels, tabular mono for numbers/durations), not a flashy hero. Should read like something a crew would actually reference on set. Follow the artifact-design skill's fundamentals (both light/dark themes via tokens, real content, no lorem) when building as an HTML artifact.

## When adapting a new source sequence

1. Read through the source poses/movements in order.
2. For each, invent a short "beat" title distinct from the literal pose name (e.g., "Seated Breath Awareness" → "Backstage Calm").
3. Assign framing + lighting that varies meaningfully across the list and tracks the sequence's energy arc.
4. Write direction as physical action only.
5. Add hold/duration proportional to the original pose's stated duration where available.
6. Close with a notes section covering wardrobe, continuity, and deliverables.
